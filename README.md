The third and final analysis project looking into how soil data effects the plant life above. This projects delved into the extremes of pH and how they affect the soil around, including classification and risk analysis.

# 🌱 Soil Systems Analysis: A Data Science Series

A multi-part data analytics project exploring soil structure, fertility, and chemical suitability using global soil datasets.

---

## 📊 Overview

This project investigates soil systems through three core dimensions:

* **Clay Content** → physical structure
* **Organic Carbon** → biological activity & fertility
* **pH Levels** → chemical balance & usability

Each analysis builds on the previous, forming a cohesive narrative that culminates in a systems-level understanding of soil quality.

---

## 🧱 Project Structure

### 1. Clay Analysis — *Soil Structure*

* Data cleaning & feature engineering
* Depth-based analysis
* Spatial distribution mapping
* Signature **depth profile curve**
* Variability analysis across soil profiles

---

### 2. Organic Carbon Analysis — *Soil Fertility*

* Distribution and depth-dependent behavior
* **Exponential decay modeling** of carbon with depth
* **Surface-to-deep carbon ratio** analysis
* Spatial mapping of high-fertility zones
* Surface vs deep soil comparisons

---

### 3. pH Analysis — *Soil Chemistry & Suitability*

* pH classification (Acidic / Neutral / Alkaline)
* Depth-based stability analysis
* Category distribution across soil layers
* **Soil suitability mapping (optimal pH range)**
* Identification of **extreme pH risk zones**

---

---

## 🚀 Key Insight

Soil quality is not defined by a single variable:

> **High clay ≠ high fertility ≠ usable soil**

Instead, soil systems must be understood through the interaction of:

* Structure (clay)
* Biology (organic carbon)
* Chemistry (pH)
